To Denmark

What is this?

While the larger wedding is happening in New Jersey on the 25th, we'll be doing something special in Denmark on the 29th with a small group of people. It's no secret, but the invited is a selected group to allow us to manage the entire size and reserve a few group events we have planned.

When are we going?

Departing late Sunday, 26 May which arrives Monday, 27 May. Please feel free to book your travel arragements. We already have our flights, so we're doing this.

What are we doing there?

Wednesday, 29 May is the day we ask for you to join us for as much of the entire day as you can. We have lots planned. We will also be organizing optional events on Monday and Tuesday and we hope you're able to join us then as well. Beginning Thursday, feel free to do your own thing but we'll still be around if you want to stay together.

Update: 10 March 2019

We're very excited you'll all be able to join us in Denmark. Please take a few moments to read through this page and then when you're ready, click on the link at the end to give us more information that will help us plan this trip.

We have three topics we want to share information with you:

  • Lodging
  • Bikes
  • Food

Wednesday is still the day we are planning on doing everything together. There are approximately 20 of us attending, and we'll be organizing visits to a few different locations, two meals, and a day filled with activity. Expect to be out until at least midnight, local time!

Lodging
  • We've found that AirBnb's are better than hotels in Copenhagen (in terms space, location, and value)
  • So we're looking to reserve 2 or 3 different AirBnb apartments for the group to share
  • Each set of travelers will have their own bedroom
  • Each AirBnb location will be strategically selected
  • If you wish to opt-out of this, or want to specify and requests, please do so on the linked form below
Bikes
  • The best way to get around the city is on bike, so we'll be taking the group to rent bikes (and accessories) on Monday afternoon
  • Biking is the fastest and cheapest way to get around (cabs are slow and expensive and the metro covers only part of the city and is also expensive)
  • In Copenhagen, The biking culture is unqiue and safe
  • But there are some rules to the road to get familiar with, particularly in regards to using hand signals
Food & Events
  • While you're more than welcome to spend Monday and Tuesday visting the city on your own, we'll be organizing a few different events and meals to do with folks that want to participate
  • You're welcome to pick and choose what works for you
  • Please express your interest for each in the sign up form
