Jay and Joan Gerish purchased what is now "Born to Run Farm" in 1978 and used the property as a family getaway home and a space to train their race horses. The Gerish’s 4 children and 7 grandchildren continue to maintain the property as their parent’s/grandparent’s had always wanted, with love and family values.

Dress Code: Dressy Casual

For the Ladies: Pull out that cute sundress, maxi-dress, jumpsuit, or fun pair of culottes with a flowy blouse! Keep the heels at home and wear a pair of wedges or dressy sandals instead. We'll be on grass for majority of the evening until we hit the dance floor!

For the Lads: Get that ironing board out and freshen up a pair of dress pants or khakis and a dress shirt. Throw in a tie or a sport jacket if you feel up for it (but it's not required)! Feel free to add in a sweater as well, but let's hope the weather doesn't call for it. A nice pair of dress shoes or dressy sneakers are fine!

To All: Dress appropriate to the weather! May can be a tricky month, and as much as we are crossing our fingers for a warm, early Summer night, you may need to bring a sweater or light jacket with you since we'll be celebrating outside!

About the area

This is a part of New Jersey that most don’t have the chance to explore. It’s a nice reminder of why it’s named the ‘Garden State’. It’s also the part where Claudette grew up! Born to Run Farm holds a special place in our hearts because it overlooks Butler’s Park Road; the road on which Claudette and her family were raised (and her parents still reside). The entire area has a lot of history, a lot of charm, and a lot of ‘small town’ feels.
